Camping at Bruneau Dunes State Park, ID
1 2 3 4 5 6


We were camping at Bruneau Dune State Park, ID during Memorial Weekend. We also went around for sightseeing.

The first place we went to was camas lily at Camas Prairie Centennial Marsh.

I want to see it for a long time and I was finally there. It was very impressive. I read that camas lily’s root is a little ball and it contains insulin. But we can’t eat it directly. We would be sick if we eat it directly. I am wondering why since we need insulin to digest the sugar. I welcome any information about this.

Native Americans eat it. They would cook it for couple of days until it changes to sugar . Next weekend, two tribes will come to harvest the bulbs.

Later on, I learned that India people also eat it. It is called "candy cane" over there.
