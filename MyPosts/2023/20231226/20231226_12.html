I am trying to answer all the questions from my friends. Our cruise was operated by Hurtigruten.

To go to Antarctica, you had to travel by ship or by plane. Flying was usually departing from Australia, spending just a few hours in Antarctica. I didn't like this option.

There were several cruise companies. I selected Hurtigruten because their ship had a bow similar to an icebreaker, which should make it better at handling ice and safer overall.

Additionally, cruise ships carrying more than 500 passengers were not allowed to land on shore — they can only do scenic cruising. That’s why I didn’t choose other bigger cruise lines.

Our itinerary: We departed from Buenos Aires, Argentina on December 28, 2023, flew to Ushuaia, boarded the ship on December 29, and disembarked on January 8, then flew back to Buenos Aires.

The entire journey involved crossing the Drake Passage, which was where two great oceans meet — the Atlantic on one side and the Pacific on the other! It was about 4,500 meters deep.

We had to sail for two days across it. We were currently on our way back, and it really was an endless expanse of ocean. The waves were quite big — I had already used up all my seasickness medication.
